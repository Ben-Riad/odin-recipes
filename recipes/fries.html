French fries recipe

Description

Homemade fries are a simple and delicious snack made by cutting potatoes into sticks, soaking them to remove excess starch, then frying them twice to achieve the perfect balance of a soft, fluffy interior and a crispy golden exterior. After the first fry at a lower temperature to cook the potatoes through, they are cooled and fried again at a higher heat to create that signature crunch. Lightly seasoned with salt—or dressed up with spices, herbs, or cheese—they’re best served hot with your favorite dipping sauces.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium potatoes (Russet or similar starchy type)
  • 2–3 cups vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • Optional: paprika, garlic powder, black pepper, or parmesan for extra flavor

Instructions

  1. Prepare the potatoes:Wash, peel (optional), and cut into sticks about 1 cm thick. Try to keep them even for uniform cooking.
  2. Soak:Place the cut potatoes in cold water for at least 30 minutes (up to 2 hours). This removes excess starch and makes them crispier.
  3. Dry:Drain and pat the potatoes completely dry with a towel—any water will cause splattering.
  4. First fry:eat oil in a deep pan to 150 °C (300 °F). Fry the potatoes in batches for about 4–5 minutes, until they’re soft but not browned. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. Rest:Let them cool for 10–15 minutes (this step helps with crispiness).
  6. Second fry:Reheat oil to 190 °C (375 °F). Fry the potatoes again in batches for 2–3 minutes until golden and crispy.
  7. Season & serve:Drain, sprinkle immediately with salt (and any spices you like), then serve hot with ketchup, mayo, or your favorite dip.
